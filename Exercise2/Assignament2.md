## 1. Main Visualization Goal (Audience)

The primary goal of this visualization is to **illustrate the distribution of global crop production by farm size categories**. The chart communicates how much of the total agricultural output is generated by farms of different sizes. The intended audience includes **researchers, policymakers, agricultural economists, students, and anyone interested in understanding global food production dynamics**.

---

## 2. Visualization Dimensions Using a Visualization Wheel

According to the visualization wheel framework:

- **Data type:** Quantitative proportions (percentages of total production) and categorical grouping (farm size categories).  
- **Analytical task:** Comparison — the visualization allows the viewer to assess how different farm sizes contribute to overall crop production.  
- **Representation type:** Infographic/bar/percentage chart — showing share distribution across categories.  
- **Goal:** Communicate how production is distributed across farm sizes, facilitating understanding of structural differences in agricultural systems globally.

The visualization emphasizes **comparative proportions** rather than temporal trends, making it suitable for exploring structural relationships in agricultural production.

---

## 3. Main Drawbacks Following Alberto Cairo and Edward Tufte

**Alberto Cairo’s principles** recommend clear, truthful, and functional visualizations. In this chart:

- A possible drawback is **lack of precise numerical labels** for each category, which could make it harder for the viewer to extract exact values without inference.  
- If many color shades are used without clear labeling, it may reduce readability.

**Edward Tufte’s principles** emphasize maximizing data‑ink ratio and avoiding chartjunk.

- If decorative elements (icons, background patterns) are present, they might distract from the data interpretation.  
- The visualization should keep **minimal non‑data visuals** and focus on proportion information.

Improving the chart by adding clear value labels and reducing unnecessary decorations would increase clarity and align better with Tufte’s data‑ink ratio principle.

---

## 4. Graphical Variables Used and Their Suitability

The visualization uses the following graphical variables:

- **Color:** Different farm size categories are distinguished by color.  
- **Area/Length/Position:** The size of each segment or bar represents the share of production attributed to that category.  
- **Shape:** Segments in the chart help distinguish categories visually.

These graphical encodings are **suitable** for comparing proportions because:

- **Position and area** are perceptually effective for comparing quantitative shares.  
- **Color** distinguishes categories without distorting the quantitative interpretation, as long as the palette is chosen for contrast and accessibility.

Overall, the variables support clear comparison across categories, but could be improved with direct numeric annotations.
